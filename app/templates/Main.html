Our Project Timeline

Data Collection & Transformation

We had to find, vet, and download data sets needed to make our model. To transform it into a useable form, we lemmatized and vectorized the text and removed stop words, grammar, etc.

Data Cleaning

We grabbed our dataset from kaggle. Our dataset consisted of tweets from twitter, either labeled 1 or 0. 1 indicates a negative or “sad, mad” sentiment. 0 indicates a positive or “happy, neutral” sentiment. After separating the tweets into positive and negative sentiments, we split both of the categories apart and randomly shuffled them. After that we put it back together in a new, random order. We then begin tokenizing the text, while also getting rid of punctuation, lowercasing all text, getting rid of links, ‘@’ symbols, and stemming every word. After that we separated our data into a training set and a test set.

Model Architecture

We at first had 5 layers in our model, but after further testing we ended up having 3 layers. Our embedding layer had a vocabulary size of 500, a vector space of 8, and an input length of 35 words. Our second layer is an LSTM RNN that has 2 hidden nodes. Our last layer is a dense layer with the activation function sigmoid.

Training Model

We trained our model with our training dataset for 5 epochs (or cycles). To visualize our results we created a confusion matrix to see how accurate our model was. We ended up with a 92 percent accuracy with our training dataset.

Testing Model

We grabbed our testing data set and passed it through the model. We ended up getting an 80 percent accuracy for this test run, which is not too bad for our model. 

Deploy Model

We wrote small code to load model into a website, built the design and layout of the website, Practice presentations, and referred to our model on the website so anyone can try it out.
